Kazem Cheshmi

I am a PhD candidate at ParaMathics lab, at the department of computer science, University of Toronto, and advised by Prof. Maryam Mehri Dehnavi. My research area includes sparse computations, high-performance computing, compiler optimization, and parallel programming. I have developed the Sympiler code generator for transforming sparse linear algebra codes and the NASOQ solver for solving sparse quadratic programs.
I will join the Computer Science department of the New Jersey Institute of Technology in spring 2022. Multiple Ph.D. positions are available.
